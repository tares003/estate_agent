How to get your home ready for viewings

PS
Priya ShahSenior sales agent · Updated 14 May 2026 · 8 min read

First impressions are made in seconds. A little preparation before a viewing can be the difference between a polite "we'll think about it" and an offer the same afternoon.

You don't need to spend a fortune or rip the place apart. Most of what makes a home show well is about light, space and a sense that it's been cared for. Here's how we'd approach it.

Start at the front door

Buyers form a view before they've even stepped inside. A tidy path, a clean door, a working bell and a pot of something green go a surprisingly long way. If your hallway is the first thing they see, keep it clear — coats, shoes and post have a way of shrinking a space.

The homes that sell fastest aren't always the grandest — they're the ones that feel easy to picture living in.

Let the light in

Open every curtain and blind, and clean the windows if you can. Natural light makes rooms feel larger and warmer. In darker rooms, a couple of well-placed lamps do more than a single bright ceiling bulb.

  • Replace any dead bulbs — consistency matters more than brightness.
  • Turn lamps on for evening or winter viewings.
  • Push furniture back from windows so the light isn't blocked.

Declutter, don't depersonalise entirely

Clear surfaces and tidy storage make rooms feel bigger, but a completely stripped-back home can feel cold. Aim for "comfortable show home" — lived-in, but calm.
